Why Algorithms Get Taste Wrong

Most recommendation systems rely on the same simple idea: “People who liked A also liked B — so you will too.”

Sounds tidy. But human taste doesn’t behave tidily. Two people can watch the same film and walk away with completely opposite reactions.

  • One falls for the atmosphere.
  • Another can’t stand the main character.
  • A third is ready to scream because the pacing feels painfully slow.

On the surface, the film is identical. Inside — each person grabs onto something different.

An algorithm that only sees metadata and crowd behavior can’t catch these nuances. It predicts patterns, not meaning.

So what makes this project any different?

Instead of guessing what you “should” like, we start with what you actually felt. Your reactions — your words — your story.
